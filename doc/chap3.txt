  [33X[0;0YThe  [12XSemirings[112X  package  provides  a  means  of encoding and decoding into a
  format called [12XSemi6[112X, inspired by the [12Xgraph6[112X format.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Y[12XSemi6[112X encodes a semiring [23XS = (A,M)[123X of order [23Xn[123X (two [23Xn \times n[123X Cayley tables)
  as one short ASCII string.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe first byte is [23Xn + 63[123X, exactly as in [12Xgraph6[112X. The payload is a bit-stream:
  first  the upper triangle (diagonal included) of the symmetric table [23XA[123X, then
  the  full  table [23XM[123X. Each entry is stored in [23Xb = \lceil{\log_2 n\rceil}[123X bits,
  most-significant  bit first. Zero-bits are used for padding until the length
  is a multiple of 6. The stream is then divided into 6-bit blocks, each block
  has  63 added, and the resulting bytes (ASCII 63–126) are concatenated. Thus
  a  semiring becomes one short, string that is identical in spirit to [12Xgraph6[112X,
  but  with  a  variable‐width  per-entry field instead of the single edge-bit
  used  for  graphs.  That  is to say, the [23Xb=1[123X case effectively reduces to the
  [12Xgraph6[112X format (with an additional square table).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe order of the semiring must be between 1 and 63, inclusive.[133X
